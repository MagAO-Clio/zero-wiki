CLIO2 Support Agreement

DRAFT 2012-04-07

1 Instrument Description

Clio2 is a 1-5 micron imager, and coronagraph built to exploit the unique sensitivity and resolution of deformable secondary AO systems. A particular area of focus for the instrument is to provide sensitive detection of giant planets at 3.3, 3.8 and 4.8 microns. Clio2 uses a 512×1024 HAWAII-1 array.

The instrument was designed and built by a team at Steward Observatory including Melanie Freed, Andy Breuninger, Ari Heinze, Suresh Sivanandam, and Phil Hinz. Information about its operation on the MMT can be found at

http://zero.as.arizona.edu/groups/clio2usermanual/

2 Configuration

The following information will be provided relating to the telescope/instrument configuration:

2.1 Specify the intended port

  • Clio2 is mounted on the Clay Nasmyth East port on the Magellan AO NAS ring (not the usual Magellan guider).

2.2 Specify the secondary mirror and ADC:

  • Magellan f/16 ASM, no ADC

2.3 Provisions for guiding: Does the instrument require one of the observatory guiders and, if so, specify which model?

  • One Magellan guider/S-H assembly is built into the Magellan AO NAS ring.

2.4 Specify instrument size and weight:

The instrument cryostat with attached motors is an envelope 30 inches high, 26 inches deep, and 30 inches wide.

The instrument weighs approximately 50-60 lbs., or 120 lbs. including the mount.

2.5 List size and location of instrument racks, compressors, and other equipment.

The Clio instrument is configured with an electronics rack that houses the power supplies, instrument server, and network switches. The rack can be thermally controlled with glycol. It is designed to operate on the floor of the E. Nasmyth platform approximately 25 ft from the NAS. The rack model is TBD .

2.6 Description and location of the control console. Is this dedicated equipment or shared with other instruments?

The Clio instrument can be controlled locally via a rack mounted monitor at the electronics support rack or remotely via ssh access using remote X display. Typical operations are via the remote interface.

2.7 Discuss telescope and rotator balance considerations.

The instrument is light, but is not centered on the rotator access. We anticipate counterweights will be needed to balance the instrument, which will be built in to the ring.

Clio is currently designed to only allow +/- 90 degree rotation. This is being remedied and will be demonstrated prior to shipment. Solid nitrogen is used for detector cooling and can operate at any orientation. The modification will either involve solidifying the nitrogen in the outer vessel, or modifying the outer vessel to have a “straw” within the canister. Final details/demo. will be presented at the pre-ship review.

2.8 Specify special baffle requirements.

  • No special baffles are required.

2.9 Cable description and layout. State if the cables are permanently installed and if they are shared with other instruments. Is a cable wrap required?

Clio has a dedicated control cable bundle which supplies all required electric, signal, and control services. The cable bundle emanates from the Clio electronics rack and terminates at the instrument. The cable bundle is approximately 25 ft in length.

No cable wrap is required. A cable strain relief will be fabricated at the center of the instrument rotation to provide cable management fromt he cryostat to the electronics rack.

3 Service requirements

The following information will be provided related to controls, utilities, and cabling:

3.1 Requirements for power, compressed air, and coolant.

110VAC, 20 amps.

We will need coolant sufficient to dissipate ~500 W.

3.2 Requirements for heat extraction.

The Clio electronics rack generates approximately 500 W of heat which will need to be extracted by facility coolant.

3.3 Requirements for cryogens.

The instrument is cooled with LN2. The LN2 reservoir is filled at the fill port located at the top of the reservoir vessel. The reservoir capacity is 9 liters. The hold time for the outer vessel is approximately 24 hours, requiring refill of the outer vessel of ~ 4 liters. The hold time for the inner vessel is not certain, but we typically refill every ~4 days.

The hold time may change after upgrade of the system to be rotatable over 360 degrees.

4.5 Description of the procedures required for routine instrument changes. These include pump down and cool down procedures, cabling, power up, filter and mask preparation, preparation of the data system, and actual start-up procedures. Actual handling procedures are described in a later section.

Detailed written procedures - refer to procedural document (checklist)

  • Before cooling, Clio should be put on the turbopump. Pumping with a turbopump or similar system is required for 24 hours prior to cooling.
  • Clio needs 12 hours to completely cool down to a stable temperature. The detector is operational 6 hours after cooldown. If possible, it is thus preferable to begin cooldown the day before the Clio run starts.
  • Only the outer vessel is filled for the first fill, and this takes typically 10 minutes to get the cooling down going. Fill until the liquid spurts out of the tube.
  • Look for the vapor to stop exiting from Clio after about 30 minutes. This is an indication the liquid has boiled off. Refill the outer vessel a second time. It should be empty after around 3 hours from the start of your cooldown. Refill a third time.
  • When the outer vessel temperature is 150 degrees or below, you can fill the inner vesel. The limit of 150 degrees is important, as this is the temperature that the getter in the dewar activates. Do not fill the inner vessel if the outer vessel is above 150 K! If the detector is the coldest surface in the dewar, the detector will attract all residual vapours!
  • Align the cold stop
    Twilight sky is needed for this.
  • Close the AO loop
  • Focus Clio2

5 Troubleshooting and repair

The following information will be provided relating to troubleshooting and repair:

5.1 List the subsystems that are serviceable by the observatory staff and describe those repair procedures that may be attempted by the local staff for each subsystem.

Acceptable for troubleshooting:
Lack of coolant flow in electronics rack.
Cabling connectivity.
Cryogen levels and solid nitrogen pump connections.
Computer status.
Lakeshore detector temperature stabilization.

5.2 List the critical subsystems and repair procedures that specifically may not be attempted by the local staff.

Instrument alignment will be done by UA MagAO team.
No access to the cryostat should be attempted without consulting UA.

Primary POC is Phil Hinz. Hinz should be contact in the event of instrument failure or subsystem failures. There will be a UA team member on-call for each Clio run.

University of Arizona will authorize and pay for replacement parts.

5.5 Specify what constitutes chronic or severe problems that go beyond the ability of the staff to maintain the instrument and/or that place excessive demands on the technical staff such that normal observatory operation is impaired. 7 Handling & storage fixtures

The following information will be provided relating to handling, storage, and shipping:

7.1 Description of required handling & storage fixtures and a statement of how they are to be provided.

  • A cart is being fabricated that will provide the storage mount for Clio and allow movement of the instrument from the auxiliary building to the Nasmyth platform.
  • Two pieces of equipment are moved on 2 carts: 1) Clio and 2) E-rack with umbilical
  • In the aux. building, we need power and ethernet for the electronics rack, for remote access to the machine.
  • Prior to a run, Clio should be cooled and pumped 1-2 days before the run in the aux. building - need cryo and turbo pump in storage location.

7.2 Procedures for moving the instrument to and from the telescope and mounting it on the telescope.

  • Instrument will be rolled from storage location onto the elevator to bring it to the Nasmyth platform. Once on the platform, the overhead crane will be used to lift the cryostat from the cart to the NAS.
  • Installation described in MAOP-039.

7.3 Description of the off-telescope storage requirements including amount of space, type of environment, and power or cooling.

  • The cart and rack both occupy a relatively small foot print of several square meters. While off the telescope, it is preferable to have the electronics rack attached to power and network for access to the instrument computer. Each unit will include wheels to facilitate transport to and from the Nasmyth Platform and the storage location.

Cart Dimensions: 62 in (158) H, 42 in (107) W, 17 in (43) D.
Electronics Enclosure Dimensions: 48 in (122) H, 23 in (58) W, 25 in (64) D.
